## thoughts

Alright. I know there are lots of SWEs hating and saying oh, this isn't production grade...

Well shut the fuck up -- I don't care. Not every piece of software needs zillions of users and petabytes of data. This is a good example of a need/idea I've had on the back of my mind but haven't had the time of day to make it work as I hate frontend & UI stuff. Ignoring how crappy the emails are formatted/look and the buggy button for the AI voice clone, I am impressed by this one-shot tool w/ a few tweaks on my end. Is it the most secure piece of code? NO but it doesn't matter again -- it's just to calm my dog down when I'm away w/ family/friends. 

Global solutions and massive datastores really are superfluous and so distant/above and beyond for your average joe. We continually add meta-level this and meta-meta-level that and toss and turn frameworks, languages, practices, etc. -- it's so exhausting and don't even get me started on the high-brow know-it-all devs that cringe internally if you aren't using emacs, nixos, and the other coolest edgelord tech. AI is so refreshing and a nice add-on to search. Yes, perhaps I could have searched and found this on stackoverflow or github, but this saves a lot of time and allows me to personalize important things without worrying about the syntax/details. In this sense, AI acts as a compiler from english to machine-code and is fucking fabulous except that it's fuzzy and doesn't make the best judgement calls / read your mind (which is OK). 

Is this a billion-dollar industry, IDK? But having open-source variations is awesome and I'm looking forward to this more declarative, local form of programming. IDGAF about all the concurrency and testing and yadayadah -- catch me instead climbing rocks, spending time with family, etc. and my. half-assed vibe-coded apps. Of course, I enjoy programming and love algorithms and math but I view AI as a tool to help us here not harm us -- it doesn't actually commoditize intelligence because you need to know what you're querying for in the first place to get a great product. Saying "you are an expert programmer..." as a CEO who has never written a line of code in their life will not generate quality products and the same for SWEs who do not know ML or math -- this isn't going to replace your knowledge of stochastic calculus and all the refined tastes you have accumulated but simply supplement that. 

HOWEVER I am not looking forward to the enshittification of this tech which is bound to happen as these companies are valued in the BILLIONS... Like what's going to need to happen to justify those prices are 1) ads and SEO (fucking awful - already see it in GPT free...), 2) workers being replaced or "more productive" (rat race speeds up!), and/or 3) transformative tech / faster research and automation (unlikely given verification needed and these are fuzzy searches at the end of the day).
