## consumerism

> Consumerism... is characterized by the continuous acquisition of goods and services in ever-increasing quantities. In contemporary consumer society, the purchase and the consumption of products have evolved beyond the mere satisfaction of basic human needs, transforming into an activity that is not only economic but also cultural, social, and even identity-forming [[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consumerism).

> Knowledge is an awareness of facts, a familiarity with individuals and situations, or a practical skill. Knowledge of facts, also called propositional knowledge, is often characterized as true belief that is distinct from opinion or guesswork by virtue of justification [[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge).

## What would science look like if it was not driven by consumerism?

> Science is a systematic discipline that builds and organises knowledge in the form of testable hypotheses and predictions about the universe [[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science)

It is inherently based on pushing boundaries albeit at increasingly lower rates of return. At some point (a point I believe already occurred), we are beyond the "mere satisfaction of basic human needs" and spoiling ourselves by sounding smart and writing yet another pretentious paper about some obscure topic that will not help anyone. 

The inherent issue we face is that of deciding value. If we already have the materials needed to be equible, then how do we allocate these resources. It could be the loudest that gets them or the ones that complain the most or the youth or the old or all sorts of measures that have pros and cons. If we look at related species of animals like lions or bears, they face similar challenges with allocating sparse resources to larger communities where there are established hierarchies for who gets what. Even for plant-eating species, we see similar hierarchies being formed [[4]](https://escholarship.org/content/qt4s2986k7/qt4s2986k7_noSplash_0bedf44311d1ddee319a4a400e94c280.pdf). There are submissive and dominant sub-cultures that are created and end up deciding how to allocate resources.
