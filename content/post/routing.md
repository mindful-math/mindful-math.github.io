Commentary on [this routing paper](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9055440). 
<!--more-->

## thoughts

* problem: optimization of service placement and routing of requests in dense graphs with multidimensional constraints
* propose a randomized routing technique to achieve near-optimal performance
* I don't like the breakdown by special cases but there is a heuristic (SPR algo) for approximating the intractable general problem
* Using rounding + probabilities to find where to place requests
* we really need caching, communication & computation for agentic scientific discovery but this bypasses the hypothesis generation itself and how to reroute through traffic should a request fail

## other thoughts

* I think science is driven by pairs of older distninguished researchers paired with newer and up and coming ones. The newer person with less context is able to look at things with a fresh perspective and criticize what exists with what he knows about physical reality or believes to be true. The experienced researcher is able to filter these many ideas generated by younger folk and highlight / work off the interesting hypotheses. This is the tradeoff of velocity paired with crystallized intelligence of older faculty. 
* If we have a bunch of old faculty / AI, then we cannot make progress and reach steady states. Many companies circumvent this by continually hiring/firing to promote younger folk entering and making required changes to adapt to the changing environment.
