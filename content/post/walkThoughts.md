Some thoughts on work while walking around a pond. 
<!--more-->

## thoughts

- It would be interesting to organize work/housing like school. Instead of just having enough money, there would be behavior and skill-based matching for the location based on needs. This would encourage local diversity like schools have which I think is quite lacking in workplaces. This would require schools to align a bit with workplace needs so that people are not totally left behind. Federal government could incentivize companies to move to certain locations and strip down the global, one-size-fits-all companies we have currently rulling the US (the tricky part because greedy politicians + judges are attached to capitalism). This would also drop US power relative to other countries (surprised Pikachu face); but at the same time, this encourages trade and trust. I think we could also incentivize automation to help people work less not more; the main issue is consumption is at an all time high and doesn't seem to show signs of stopping as seen by global leaders and their behaviors. However, I think the general populace are fed up and would like more simple, less career-oriented lives w/ more meaningful interactions with peers rather than screen time.

- Glass is pretty close to water and its reflective properties are quite interesting and why I think I like contemporary modern styles. It's transparency and reflection that I just love; if you have those things in order, you could daze hours into space just appreciating the natural beauty of the world around you

- Alongside the above, I think we just need gradual, slow, and conscious changes in behavior and attitude to get to where we want to go as a country. We do not need drastic reforms and revolutions as media tries to stir up and promote. We just need quick discussions where we listen and make small, but directive changes the local populations and communities need to be a better version of themselves. But even that is misconstrued - we don't need "better", we just need appreciation and to slow down a touch and smell some grass as they say.
