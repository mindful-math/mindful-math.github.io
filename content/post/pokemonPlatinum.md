Using LLMs to finish [Pokémon Platinum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Platinum).
<!--more-->

## setup
I was just curious how fast I could finish the game with a certain team composition / aesthetic & use Gemini 3 (Fast) to guide me through the process. TLDR: it took 2 days & had mixed results.

## improvements
> they don't like tail-end queries

The models have probability distributions at the end of the day. Each query is a sample of the distribution. In my case, I got constantly recommended the same cookie-cutter team: Staraptor, Garchomp, Roserade, Staraptor, Floatzel, Bibarel, etc. IT WAS SO FRUSTRATING trying to get it to use anyone out of the ordinary - Togekiss, Yanmega, etc. even after explicitly calling for these. I would ask it to tabularize movesets for my team and it would drop these unordinary ones without me asking and swap in for regulars. WTF... I think Gemini needs context like Claude Code for a session like a set of axioms always fed in that shall not be disobeyed. Querying Gemini is just like finding Pokemon in the wild - there are probabilities attached to each response and you have to be patient and keep trying to match your expectations. At the end of the day, I think my brain works better for strategy, planning, etc. and LLMs are subjected to the law of large numbers - mediocrity & frequency dominate the outputs. This will be an SEO NIGHTMARE soon enough - we can't have these technologies and not have people poison them with their own companies, ideologies, etc. I see this through my question - what's the best team? They always respond with cookie-cutter reddit posts / what's online and do not take into context my preferences against the data. There should be a way to cut out that data in between and just align my preferences against the raw stats and database of Pokemon available. Anything in between is poison & will impede results. This is a great metaphor for what is about to come - give me each company's raw stats & let's create a transparent price-optimal marketplace (like HFT) NOT some subjective loudest in the room BS generator (where I think it's going)...

> advertising & SEO...

I am scared for these two poisons. When this happens (and it's already starting), this will be much worse than the web. It will create echo chambers for ideologies & brainwash masses into wasting money on inferior products. Why can't we just include raw stats on products & compare accurately i.e. Amazon (despite how much I hate their culture up top)

## surprises
* For simple queries like where can I get TM07 or rare candies, these are great (still not sure if it beats guides online when advertising in LLMs kicks in...)
* After I asked explicitly, it was able to give decent cookie-cutter movesets for a team. It knew the strengths, weaknesses, and how to improve my team. It could be likened to a second brain to bounce ideas off and get feedback on your work.
* To beat the game took two days - I think though Google would suffice here but ads on search suck so much these days... perhaps Brave would work but it's hard to query.
