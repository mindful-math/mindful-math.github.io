  1. If $X$ and $Y$ are two independent exponential random variables with different rates, then

$$P(X+Y\leq k) = \int_0^k P(X+Y=l)dl = \int_0^k\int_0^l P(X=x)P(Y-l-x)dxdl $$

$$ = \int_0^k \alpha\beta e^{-\beta l}\int_0^l e^{-(\alpha-\beta)x}dxdl = \int_0^k \frac{\alpha\beta}{\beta - \alpha} e^{-\beta l}\left(e^{-(\alpha - \beta)l} - 1\right)dl$$

$$ = \frac{\alpha}{\alpha-\beta}\left(1-e^{-\beta k}\right) + \frac{\beta}{\beta-\alpha}\left(1-e^{-\alpha k}\right)$$

Differentiating with respect to $k$ yields the desired result:

$$ \frac{d}{dk} P(X+Y\leq k) = P(X+Y=k) = \frac{\alpha\beta}{\beta - \alpha}\left(e^{-\alpha k} - e^{-\beta k}\right).$$
